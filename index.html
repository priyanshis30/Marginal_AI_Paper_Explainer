What it does in the margin

Five kinds of notes, written where you need them.

Not a chatbot bolted onto a PDF viewer — every note sits next to the line it explains, the way a sharp classmate would annotate your printout.

01 — plain language

Explains like you're 15

Jargon gets a translation the first time it shows up, without dumbing down the actual idea.

Appears as a red note beside the paragraph.

02 — equations

Highlights equations

Every symbol gets named, and the line is rewritten in words before the math.

Appears as a blue note beside the formula.

03 — figures

Explains figures

Charts and diagrams get a walkthrough of what each axis, arrow, and color means.

Anchored to the figure caption.

04 — sections

Summarizes each section

A two-line recap waits at the end of every section, so you can skim and still follow.

Appears as a green note at section breaks.

05 — recall

Quizzes you

A short quiz at the end checks what stuck, with explanations for anything you miss.
